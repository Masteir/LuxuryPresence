Frequently Asked Questions

Unclutter and organize your home Neatly arrange drawers and cabinets Keep pets outdoors or off the premises Play soft music

Declutter and Clean: Remove excess items and clean your home thoroughly to make it more appealing to buyers. Make Repairs: Address any minor repairs or maintenance issues to enhance the property’s condition. Stage Your Home: Consider staging your home or using professional photography to highlight its best features. Get a Pre-Inspection: A pre-inspection can identify potential issues that may arise during the buyer’s inspection, allowing you to address them beforehand.

Stay Organized: Keep all required documents, such as proof of identity, income verification, and bank statements, readily available. Communicate Regularly: Maintain open communication with your real estate agent, lender, and attorney to stay updated on the process. Review Documents Early: Carefully review all closing documents ahead of time to address any questions or discrepancies before the closing day. Secure Financing: Ensure your mortgage approval is finalized and that all necessary funds for closing are readily available.
